- India's youth unemployment remains one of the most pressing socioeconomic challenges.
- The country possesses a significant demographic dividend with young people entering the workforce annually.
- A major concern is the mismatch between educational qualifications and industry requirements, leading to higher unemployment rates for educated youth.
- The formal sector struggles to absorb rapidly expanding workforce, forcing many into informal or precarious employment.
- Industries such as agriculture, construction, retail, and low-skilled services continue to employ a substantial share of young workers with limited prospects for growth.
- Gender disparities contribute to the crisis, with lower labor force participation rates and higher unemployment levels among women.
